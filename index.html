Meet the Founder

Where Academic Rigour Meets Corporate Reality

AttiNexis was born out of a deeply personal insight — watching talented graduates struggle not because of a lack of knowledge, but a lack of corporate readiness. With a PhD and over a decade of hands-on corporate exposure, our founder brings both academic depth and real-world credibility to every program.

She engages directly with college leadership, TPOs, and faculty — understanding each institution's unique placement challenges and co-designing solutions that work. This isn't a standardised off-the-shelf product; it's a partnership built on trust and measurable outcomes.

"Every student deserves to graduate not just with a degree, but with the clarity, confidence, and career strategy to excel — that's the gap AttiNexis was built to close."

The Challenge

Why Student Employability Is At Risk

India's engineering and management graduates face a widening gap between what colleges teach and what employers expect. AttiNexis addresses each of these challenges head-on.

01

Skills Don't Match Jobs

According to NASSCOM, 45% of graduates are unemployable due to lack of industry-relevant, hands-on skills.

02

No Career Direction

Students lack structured guidance, leading to misaligned course and career choices that hurt long-term prospects.

03

Generic Curriculum

One-size-fits-all learning fails to address individual aspirations or specific industry needs.

04

No Real Industry Link

Limited employer collaboration means students miss real-world exposure, internship pipelines, and live project experience.

05

Rising Career Anxiety

Students fear being left behind — unsure of what to learn, how to prepare, or which career path to choose.

06

Policy Pressure Rising

NEP 2020 demands that colleges integrate employability and skill development into mainstream education — urgently.
